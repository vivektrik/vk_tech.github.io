The National Institute for the Empowerment of Persons with Visual disabilities [Divyangjan] is a premier Institute in the field of visual disability working under the administrative control of ministry of social justice and empowerment government of India. The Institute is committed to promote rights and dignity of persons with visual imparements In order to achieve this laudable objective, the Institute produces trained manpower for providing quality education, vocational training and rehabilitation services. to visually imparement persons.

It also undertakes research and developmental activities ensuring emergence of disability inclusive policies, programmes and practices. Its R&D activites have contributed a number of useful tools and enabling technologies for equal participation by the visually impared persons in different walks of life.

The Institute is largest producer and distributer of braille literature and devices in the country including talking books.

The NIEPVD has its headquarters at 116 Rajpur Road Dehradun with one Regional Centre at Chennai, Tamil Nadu, established in 1988 and two Regional Chapters at Kolkata, W.B. and Secunderabad, Andhra Pradesh, established in in the year 1997. These Chapters provide peripheral services and are being served by a small component of officers and staff transferred from the headquarters whereas Regional Centre at Chennai is a full fledged Institute with staff strength of 30 headed by a Regional Director. The Institute also coordinates and supervises Composite Regional Centre for persons with disablities. Sundarnagar H.P. established in the year 2001.
